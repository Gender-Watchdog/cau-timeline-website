### ❌ ERROR 1 — WRONG KOREAN TEXT FOR THE MOTTO (High Priority)

All documents use: **"의에 죽고 진리에 살자"**
Correct Korean text: **"의에 죽고 참에 살자"**

참 (cham) = truth, genuineness, authenticity
진리 (jinli) = truth/principle (a different word)

The correct translation is therefore: **"Die in Justice, Live in Truth"**
NOT "Die in Justice, Live in Righteousness"

Evidence: Korean Constitutional Court blog (Nov 26, 2015) quotes the original
4.19 Revolution-era placard: "의에 죽고 참에 살자". Ohmynews (Apr 14, 2015)
also uses this form. CAU's own activist tradition uses 참, not 진리.
Source: https://blog.daum.net/c_court/919

**Oldest film program in Korea:** Qualified claim only.

film program. The claim "oldest film program" is broadly made by CAU but is not
independently verified as definitively the oldest. Safer phrasing: "one of Korea's
oldest film programs (est. 1959, Theatre and Film)."

**GSAIM (첨단영상대학원):** Korea's only graduate film/imaging school to run all
four phases of the BK21 government prestige funding program. Confirmed by GSAIM's
own English-language website.
Source: https://gsaim.cau.ac.kr

Park Beom-hoon gave Woori Bank exclusive rights to operate all banking services on
CAU's two campuses in exchange for a 10 billion won (~$9M USD) "donation." Prosecutors

**Source (confirmed):** Korea Times (May 1, 2015):
https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/southkorea/20150501/chung-ang-probe-spills-to-doosan-woori

### Doosan Tower Commercial Unit (2011)

Park Beom-hoon received a 300-million-won commercial unit in Doosan Tower, central

**Source (confirmed):** Korea Times (May 1, 2015) — same URL as above

Park Beom-hoon received 1.8 billion won (~$1.6M USD) from Doosan through a front

**Source (confirmed):** Korea Times (May 1, 2015) — same URL as above

**Arrest:** May 1, 2015, following 19 hours of questioning
